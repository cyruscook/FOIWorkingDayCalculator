Response Due Date Calculator

This calculator will help you calculate the legal time limits for a public authority to respond to a request under the UK's Freedom of Information Act 2000.

While every attempt has been made to properly account for working and non-working days, this calculator may be inaccurate and neither it's results nor anything on this page should be taken as legal advice.

Requests made under other information access law, such as the Environmental Information Regulations 2004 or the Freedom of Information (Scotland) Act 2002, are not supported.

About working days

Freedom of Information requests must be responded to:

  • Promptly, and
  • Within 20 working days (with some exceptions)

The 20 working day period starts on the day following the day the organisation received the request. The organisation must then respond by midnight on the 20th working day. If you are asked to clarify your request, the 20 working day period would start the day after the organisation receives the clarification.

Days that do not count as working days are:

  • Saturdays
  • Sundays
  • New Year's Day
  • 2 January (1973 and onwards)
  • 3 January, if New Year's Day fell on a Saturday or Sunday that year (1973 and onwards)
  • Saint Patrick's Day (17 March)
  • 18 March, if Saint Patrick's Day fell on a Sunday
  • Good Friday (the last Friday before Easter Sunday)
  • Easter Monday (the day after Easter Sunday)
  • The first Monday in May
  • The last Monday in May
  • The first Monday in August
  • The last Monday in August
  • Saint Andrew's Day (30 November)
  • The first Monday after 30 November, if Saint Andrew's Day fell on a Saturday or Sunday that year
  • Christmas Day (25 December)
  • Boxing Day (26 December)
  • 27 December, if Christmas Day fell on a Saturday or Sunday that year
  • Other days, by royal proclamation (will be published in the London Gazette, e.g. 4388378)
  • Other days, as ordered by the Secretary of State for Northern Ireland (will be published in the Belfast Gazette, e.g. B-7164-1101/24)

A list of days that have been proclaimed to either be or not be bank holidays can be found here.
